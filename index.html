Marcus Aurelius

Memento mori -Meditations

Roman emperor from 161 to 180 AD and a Stoic philosopher.

"The last of the Five Good Emperors of Rome"

  • Born in April 26, 121 - Rome, Italy.
  • Predecessor of Antoninus Pius.
  • He appointed Commodus his son, as his successor.
  • Marcus showed enthusiasm for wrestling and boxing.
  • He married her cousin, Faustina the Younger.
  • In year 136, Marcus began his training in oratory.
  • After Antoninus died in 161, The Senate would soon grant him the name Augustus and the title imperator.
  • Like many emperors, Marcus spent most of his time addressing matters of law such as petitions and hearing disputes.
  • The Antonine Plague started in Mesopotamia in 165 or 166 at the end of Lucius's campaign against the Parthians. The plague also spread through Eastern Han empire of China. "Fever, diarrhea and inflammation of the pharynx, along with dry or pustular eruptions of the skin after nine days" were among the symptoms. It is believed that the plague was smallpox.
  • Marcus Aurelius died at the age of 58 on 17 March, 180 of uknown causes in his military quarters.
  • Between 170 and 180, Marcus wrote his Meditations in greek as a source for his own guidance and self-improvement. He had a logical mind, and his notes were representative of Stoic philosophy and spirituality.
  • Marcus acquired the reputation of a philosopher king within his lifetime.

Meditations is a series of personal writings by Marcus Aurelius, Roman Emperor from AD 161 to 180, recording his private notes to himself and ideas on Stoic philosophy.

They are basically the moral tenets of Stoicism, learned from Epictetus: the cosmos is a unity governed by an intelligence, and the human soul is a part of that divine intelligence and can therefore stand, if naked and alone, at least pure and undefiled, amid chaos and futility.

Marcus was a statesman, perhaps, but one of no great calibre; nor was he really a sage.

But his personal nobility and dedication survive the most remorseless scrutiny; he counted the cost obsessively, but he did not shrink from paying it.

"The soul becomes dyed with the colour of its thoughts."
